### Curriculum — subjects (11)
1. Art & Design — Developing creativity, visual thinking and artistic skills through making and responding to art.
2. Cambridge Global Perspectives™ — Building critical thinking, research, collaboration and communication skills around global issues.
3. Computing — Exploring computational thinking, programming, data and digital problem-solving.
4. Digital Literacy — Developing confident, safe and responsible use of digital technologies and online environments.
5. English (First or Second Language) — Developing reading, writing, speaking and listening skills for confident communication.
6. Mathematics — Building number sense, problem-solving, reasoning and mathematical fluency.
7. Music — Exploring rhythm, melody, performance and listening through active music-making.
8. Physical Education — Promoting physical fitness, teamwork, coordination and a love of movement and sport.
9. Science — Developing curiosity and understanding of the natural world through investigation and enquiry.
10. Wellbeing (NEW) — Supporting mental, emotional and social wellbeing to help learners thrive in and out of school.

H2: Measuring <em>Potential</em> and Progress

02 · Provide Feedback — Teacher-led formative assessment guidance helps give meaningful feedback on skill development throughout the academic year.
03 · Monitor Progress — Cambridge Primary Progression Tests assess knowledge, skills and understanding in English, Mathematics and Science across Stages 3, 4, 5 and 6.
04 · Check Achievement — Cambridge Primary Checkpoint monitors performance at the end of the programme, with learners receiving a statement of achievement and a diagnostic feedback report.

### Assessment section
H2: Three Ways to Take Your <em>A Level</em>
Body: Cambridge offers three flexible assessment routes, giving students and schools the choice of how to structure the A Level programme.

Assessment blocks (repurpose the blocks as the 3 routes):
Route 1 · AS Level Only — Students take Cambridge International AS Level only. The syllabus content is half a Cambridge International A Level. This option typically takes one year.
Route 2 · Two-Stage Route — Students take AS Level in one exam series and A Level in a later series. This staged route typically takes two years and allows students to bank AS results.
Route 3 · Full A Level — Students take all papers for Cambridge International A Level in the same exam series, usually at the end of a two-year course — the most common route at JPIC.
Note (no number) · Linear Assessment — All exams take place at the end of the course, giving learners more time to develop deep subject understanding before assessment.
