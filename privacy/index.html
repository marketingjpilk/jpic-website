Privacy Policy

Last updated: 17 May 2026

1. Introduction

St. John Paul II International College ('JPIC', 'we', 'us', or 'our') is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect personal information when you visit jpeducation.net or submit an enquiry or application through our website.

By using our website, you consent to the practices described in this Privacy Policy.

2. Information We Collect

We collect personal information only when you voluntarily provide it to us. This occurs when you:

  • Submit an enquiry through our contact or admissions enquiry form
  • Submit a formal application for admission through our online application system
  • Send us an email directly

The information we may collect includes:

  • Parent or guardian name, email address, and phone number
  • Child's name, date of birth, and current grade or year level
  • School of current attendance
  • Any additional information you choose to provide in a message field

We do not collect sensitive personal information (such as financial details, national ID numbers, or medical information) through our website.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information you provide solely for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your admissions enquiry
  • To process and manage your child's application for admission
  • To contact you with information relevant to your enquiry or application
  • To improve our admissions process and website

We do not use your personal information for marketing purposes unrelated to your enquiry, and we do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for commercial purposes.

4. How Your Information Is Stored

Enquiry and application data submitted through this website is securely stored in our admissions management system (AdmissionsHQ), which is operated and maintained by St. John Paul II International College. Access to this data is restricted to authorised school staff involved in the admissions process.

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, or misuse.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal information submitted through our website for as long as is necessary to process your enquiry or application, and for a reasonable period thereafter for record-keeping purposes consistent with Sri Lankan educational regulations. If your application is successful and your child enrols at JPIC, your information becomes part of the student record and is retained in accordance with our school data retention policy.

6. Cookies

Our website uses cookies to improve your browsing experience. Cookies are small text files stored on your device. We use:

  • Essential cookies: necessary for the website to function correctly
  • Analytics cookies: to understand how visitors use our website (if analytics are enabled)

You can control cookie settings through your browser. Disabling certain cookies may affect website functionality.

7. Third-Party Services

Our website uses the following third-party services which may process data on our behalf:

  • Cloudflare — website hosting and security (cloudflare.com)
  • Web3Forms — contact form submission processing (web3forms.com)
  • Google Maps — embedded maps on our contact page (google.com/maps)
  • Google Fonts — typography (fonts.google.com)

Each of these services operates under their own privacy policy. We recommend reviewing their policies if you have concerns.

8. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of any inaccurate personal information
  • Request deletion of your personal information, subject to our legal obligations
  • Withdraw consent for us to contact you regarding an enquiry

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below.

9. Children's Privacy

Our website is operated by a school and is intended for use by parents and guardians on behalf of their children. We do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children under the age of 18 without parental consent. All enquiry and application forms are designed to be completed by a parent or guardian.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. Updates will be posted on this page with a revised date. We encourage you to review this page periodically.
